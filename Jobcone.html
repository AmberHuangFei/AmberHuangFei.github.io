    • Struggle are unseperable with job hunt

    I was inspired to look at a possible solution to benefit first-time job seekers, I took my initiative on new grad students as my primary user group. Job hunting is tricky, and people might overlook a lot of rules. By researching their job hunting habits like planning their progress,summarizing insights and making connections. I came up with this project that will help improve their professionality and efficiency in job hunts.

    Research

    Interview

    Two sets of interview questions were prepared for this research. One of the sets is prepared for people who are still in their job-seeking journey. I aim to identify their common application behaviour, progress, and pain points; on the other hand, the second set of questions is prepared for people who have completed their job hunt. My goals are to understand what they did and what could be improved.

    Job-seeker
  • Do you have a job hunting plan?
  • What do you think you need during your job hunting?
  • Will you use any tool to keep track of your job hunting process?
  • If you are using any app to keep track what feature is missing?
  • What is your expectation? and frustration?
    • Employed
  • What method do you use to keep track of your job appliaction?
  • What is your job hunting habit?
  • What seems to be missing from the method you use?
  • If you are using any app to keep track what feature is missing?
  • How would you go about you job search differently?

    • Interview analysis

    Thruough interviewing multiple participants, I have summarized two different type of user groups.

    Raider
  • Mass Application with 0 plan
  • Lack research on the company
  • Lack organization to places they applied to Hope luck will strik them instead of having a set goal
  • Not prepare before they go into interview
  • Not organize their resume and coverletter with application
  • Constanly feel frustrated and unmotivated during job hunting
    • Sniper
  • Less application but very percise with the places they choose
  • Comprehensive research on the company before they apply
  • Organized they documents and notes
  • Know how to go about their application
  • Have a positive mindsets even if they get rejected or ghosted
  • Have a clear goal where they want to be and a career path

    • Insights

    The exsiting solution is only a info dump most job seeker don’t find it useful.
    Support for job seeker to monitor and manage their application is a missing.
    Job hunter found themselves not doing enough research on the company before application
    Motivation is gradually lost and self-doubt increase through the job hunting process, and becomes a obstacle.

    Ideation

    HMW improve the monitor and managing aspact for designer job seeker and provide them with guidence and motivation.

    Design & Testing

    Test Method

    The wireframe went through 2 rounds of user testing, method used

  • Fly on the wall
  • A/B testing

    • Test Fiding

    Too much information on the dashboard are becoming a info dump for users.
    Information in the job detail needs more improvements. Organization and structering is weak.
    More calls to action need to be applied to the App the encourage users to interact with the information.
    The interface is not clean enough for users to go through, find a better method to arrange the information and don’t put all the information on one page. It’s too much for users to go through at once.
    Cut out areas that don’t need to be on a separate page and make them into a dash/ smaller section.

    Test Improvement

    Cleaning up dashboard information gives users more freedom to adjust what is most important to them. This implementation has reduced frustration by 40%.
    Restructuring information in the job info by sectioning. Users had reflected the page is more scannable and easier to spot information.
    Providing side pop-ups to highlight the update and provide quick access, 80% of the user would follow the pop-up and instructions through testing.
    Combining sections that share similar functionality and leveraging their importance, I came up with a much easier flow for 96% of my participants to complete the test.

    High-fidelity design

    Track your progress

    Auto categorizes for applications in progress. Keep your focus on the ongoing progress.

    Research and document job detail

    Take away
    Extract insights from two user groups

    At the beginning of this project, I was only going to research with new grad students still in job hunting progress, but I realized the importance of learning the"Success case," which means If I combine the insights from students who have already acquired their opportunity, I will have two data sets and compare the results. I learned that striving for extensive data coverage. Cross-examining the results could help develop more exclusive insight and support the ideation.

    Improvment
    How would I take it to the next level?

    The next step for this project is to develop more wireframes to complete the user flow for various scenarios, aiming for more optimal experiences for users. Conducting user testing to prove my design solution and collecting more data helped me understand future decisions that I could make to turn this project an easier to adapt and motivate experience for my users.
