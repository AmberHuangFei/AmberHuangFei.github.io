    • Language learning needs more support

    Learning in a foreign environment could be an exciting and challenging situation for international students. The problem is more prominent when they are put in an environment that doesn't use their native language. They felt outcasted in the learning environment and increased social stress. Moreover, they might find the learning process stress-inducing. This project is dedicated to identifying a better solution for them to allow them to be more social and engage with native speakers. Thus, creating a healthy collaborative language learning community.

    Research

    Leading assumptions

  • International students have difficulties communicating with native speakers due to a short vocabulary and lack of confidence because of their accents.
  • games will bridge the student with native speakers
  • Communication in a group environment will increase engagement
  • Note-taking is the most effective method for students to review and back-track

    • Competitive analysis

    I went on the Duolingo website and took screenshots of the primary user flow of the learning experience. Then write down the note to complete the analysis to study the user experience closer. I also looked at the app review on App Store to understand the existing user pain point. Duolingo has been a trending “meme” on the internet, and it is an exciting research material because it reflects the user experience and attitude toward the app. The significant finding in looking at these materials helped me understand users feel pressured to keep up with their learning habits.

    Duolingo:Competitive testing

    To find out how effective Duolingo’s learning method I found a recent Duolingo user and conducted daily observations of his behaviour on the app, combined with an interview after he finished his daily learning goal. I’m able to compose this chart of the fluctuation in user experience.

    Pain Points

    Pain Point#1

    The health system in the app made user feel frustrated and stressed out in the learning process, users feel afraid to make mistakes.

    Better Approach

  • Reduce the chance for users to have accidental mistakes.
  • Motivate people by giving positive feedback instead of reminding them of their mistakes.

    • Pain Point#2

    Course has too many restrictions and is not flexible enough. The learning experience is dull. The fun factor is low and drives users away.

    Better Approach

  • Users have more power to choose their way of learning or progress.
  • Find a better learning median to encourage users’ learning motivation and passions.

    • Pain Point#3

    Reminders from the app make users feel stressed. The tone in the app is Not friendly enough to make users maintain a learning habit.

    Better Approach

  • Increase self-motivation in users learning habit.
  • Use a more subtitle method to help users to build a learning habit.

    Key insights
  • Cultural differences make it hard to build a conversation between international students with native speakers.
  • A film can be a successful medium to bridge conversation while helping reduce the cultural gap.
  • Laughter creates a social bond between people.
  • A private chat room will create a more comfortable socializing environment.
  • Note-taking is not the easiest method for the user to adapt during the film-watching process.
  • Reducing the frustration in learning helps use form their learning habit.
    • User needs
  • Building an easier way to express their difficulties.
  • Films are the most effective synchronized activity to connect students with native speakers.
  • A private chatroom/One-on-one chatroom can reduce the stress during socialization.
  • The Note-taking function needs to be more intuitive for the user to access.
  • Provide them a healthy and encouraging learning community.

    Site map

    I designed a site map to help with information architecture; with a diagram, I understood how to organize the user flow and ensure the functionality will serve its purpose by following the correct logic without breaking the user's mental models.

    Wireframe testing

    In the first round of testing, I chart up the result into a chart to help me visualize the frustration and where the obstacles occur in my wireframes. This round of testing is a milestone in my development because it helped me re-adjust my project to approach user pain points and elevate my high fidelity prototype.

    Design interation

    Before making the final design, I had made four different iterations of design and testing rounds. Here are some of the significant improvement I have made based on the test result:

    Personalized registration is king

    The registration process could add more detailed questions to help identify user interest. This project is made to be intuitive and completed with simple clicks. Iteration two has a dded more options for users to add custom answers to complete the process.

    More options to reach out

    More freedom to the user, a lot of feedback from my participants indicated adding functionality to allow them to build their personalized categories would be helpful, in the second iteration, I came up with video categories and chat that will increase users' power of interaction.

    Indication and guidance

    My participant reflected a lot of the unique functionality in the Web app is hard to understand. This problem mostly occurred in the video room and the save video part of the app, I came up with more pop up information and highlighted guidance to help users feel more confident while interacting.

    Communicate my solution

    Personalized Communication

    The sign-in will allow the user to customize their account setting, and personalize the content they prefer to learn about on the platform.

    Consistent Video feed

    Browse videos with ease and support users to find the most trending video topics. Like and save the video to the library for later view.

    Enjoy videos with partners.

    Watch videos with a partner in a private chatroom. Users could share, bond and learn together. Multi-language subtitles with the dictionary for additional support.

    Easy vocabulary review

    Review saved vocabulary and learn with more in-depth information. Get motivated by the study report and build your confidence from here.

    Connecting and bonding

    Find helpful information from call history and friend lists, add friends and build a deeper social connections.

    Take away
    How do we understand people better?

    Secondary research is more helpful than I expected. By reading professional studies on people's learning behaviour leading up to in-depth design terms. I was able to come up with solution that follows the user psychology and help they feel more at ease during each of my testing.

    User research is not only about collecting massive data. For the user research of this project, I had pay more thought into crafting my question and making sure I'm asking the right questions. During the interview, sessions noting down specific scenario is key to create a grounded user case, and with a good use case I was able to come up with a specific solution that is effective to their pain points.

    Improvement
    How would I take it to the next level?

    PAROLI is the first big project that I completed on my own. The biggest problem I first encountered is having "too much freedom", it is easy to have the project go out of scope. I find it frustrating when I realize I can develop a better solution, but time does not allow me to continue with more testing. I had learned to control my project scope and focues on a specific user flow instead of trying to jam all the possible solutions into one project.

    Having more opportunities to commiunitcate with more professionals and get more critiques from a third person point of view, I would be able to spot more issues or possibilities in this project. Moreover, I could take this project to the next level.
